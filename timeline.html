Alice Marie Johnson

In 2018, President Donald Trump commuted the sentence of Alice Marie Johnson, a nonviolent drug offender. She was convicted in 1996 for her involvement in a Memphis cocaine trafficking organization and sentenced to life imprisonment. In June 2018, after serving 21 years in prison, she was released from the Federal Correctional Institution, Aliceville, after President Donald Trump commuted her sentence.

Posthumous pardon to Jack Johnson

Johnson the first black heavyweight boxing champion, was convicted of taking his white girlfriend across state lines under the Mann Act in 1913. Critics say this law was used in racially motivated prosecutions of African-Americans. Lawyers for the Justice Department at the time argued that Johnson's relationship with a white woman was a "crime against nature." It took less than two hours for an all-white jury to convict the boxing legend. President Donald Trump has granted him full posthumous pardon in 2018
